FAQs

What is Hackathon?

Hackathon is basically made from words Hack & Marathon. Traditionally Hack means use things in such a way that they are not intended. A hackathon is event where teams or individual can participate and build something innovative in limited amount of time.

What is a HackUWIN?

HackUWIN is University of Windsor’s Annual Hackathon, organized by the students, for the students. HackUWIN is 36 hours Hackathon organized at University of Windsor.

Does it cost anything?

It is completely FREE!! However, If you are planning for Hardware Hack, that may affect your expenses.

What should I bring?

Your Student ID, your laptop (don’t forget your charger!!), a change of clothes, sleeping bags/blankets/pillows, personal items (soap, toothbrush, toothpaste, toiletries, etc.), any required hardware for your project (maybe we will provide some, stay tuned!!). Lastly and most importantly, bring some Ideas, Imagination and Excitement!

How does application process work?

Registration will open on 1 June, 2014 and end on 5 July, 2014. We want to invite all registered students but if registrations cross 250 limit we will select according to participants profile. Selected participants will get invitation mail before 18 July, 2014.

Who can participate?

Graduates, Undergraduates and High-School students from anywhere are welcome at HackUWIN. If you don't belong to any of the above categories and really want to come, contact us at contact@hackuwin.com

Are you providing travel reimbursement?

We are planning to provide buses from near universities. Stay tuned for list of buses. If you have more than 20 participants from one place we love to provide bus from there. For Travel, mail us on travel@hackuwin.com

What should I do if I don’t have a team?

Hackuwin is great place to interact with same minded people, so you can find a team within Hackuwin or participate as an individual (as a lone wolf).

Do you provide hardware components?

No, you must bring all the required things for your project like Development Boards, Wires, Soldering Machine, ICs, etc.

How does the judging work?

Judging will take place in two categories. First, General Judges will evaluate your hack and select Top 10 hacks. Top 10 teams will give 5 min presentation at closing ceremony and judge by a panel of 5 judges

Are we allowed to build on past projects?

No, Hackathon is place where you need to build something new on the spot. So building on your existing project is not allowed. You can use Internet and Open APIs.

What should I suppose to build?

That up to you, you can build whatever you want such as websites, wearable applications, mobile applications, software, hardware hack, anything! HackUWIN is open Hackathon. If you still want a project then you can pick one the problem areas from the sponsor’s.
